In the Ideation Workshop

students transition from abstract theory to concrete project sparks.

This is a high-energy collaborative session where the goal is quantity over quality initially, followed by a rigorous "professional filter."

The workshop is divided by the three core objectives:

1. Promotion: The Utility Perspective

Here, students brainstorm how an interactive device can "sell" or "showcase."

  • The Goal: Drive engagement toward a specific product or message.
  • The Idea: Instead of a static poster, how can a user "touch" a product in 3D?
  • Interaction: A "virtual try-on" or a 3D exploded view of a product triggered by hand gestures.
  • The Professional Filter: Does the interaction distract from the brand, or enhance it?

2. Entertainment: The Play Perspective

Focusing on the "Fun Factor" and the mechanics of the experience.

  • The Goal: Pure engagement through challenge and satisfaction.
  • The Idea: Miniature 2D physics games or a VR "rhythm" challenge.
  • Interaction: High-speed input/output cycles where the user is tested on skill or timing.
  • The Professional Filter: Is the "Core Loop" (Challenge → Action → Reward) addictive and clear?

3. Art: The Experiential Perspective

Exploring unconventional methods to evoke an emotional response.

  • The Goal: Expression, reflection, or sensory exploration.
  • The Idea: A digital "mirror" that distorts based on the user's movement or a soundscape that changes with the user's proximity.
  • Interaction: Abstract, non-linear, and often surprise-based.
  • The Professional Filter: Does the aesthetic quality match the emotional intent?

The "Speed Pitch" Technique

To wrap up the workshop, students perform a Speed Pitch:

  1. 30 Seconds: Present the idea.
  2. 30 Seconds: Identify the "User" (Persona).
  3. 30 Seconds: Defend the choice of technology (VR vs. 2D vs. 3D).

Brainstorming Worksheet

Here we will create the Student Brainstorming Worksheet as a new note.

This document is designed to guide students through the four critical stages of ideation:

defining the objective, identifying the persona, mapping the interaction loop, and performing a technical reality check.

You can now use this worksheet to facilitate the Ideation Workshop part of your course.

Interactive Device Brainstorming Worksheet

STEP 1: The "What" (Core Objective)
  • Circle one: PROMOTION / ENTERTAINMENT / ART
  • The Elevator Pitch: In two sentences, what does the device do?
STEP 2: The "Who" (Persona)
  • User Profile: Give them a name and a background (e.g., "Tech-savvy Tim" or "Museum-goer Martha").
  • User Need: Why are they interacting with your device? (To be amazed? To learn? To compete?)
STEP 3: The "How" (Interaction Loop)
  • Action: What is the physical or digital trigger? (e.g., A wave, a click, a shout)
  • Response: What is the immediate sensory feedback? (e.g., A flash, a sound, a 3D movement)
  • The "Juice": One small detail to make the interaction feel "good" or "premium."
STEP 4: Technical Reality Check
  • Platform: 2D Web / 3D Immersive / Virtual Reality
  • Required Skills: What do you need to learn in the next 10 hours to make this work?
  • The MVP: If you only had 5 hours to code, what is the single most important feature?
